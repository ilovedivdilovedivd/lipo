What is liposuction?

The word liposuction can be translated from Latin as “fat suction”. In the modern beauty industry, liposuction is a sought-after cosmetic procedure that has become widespread.

Liposuction is simple in the technique of performing, is safe for patients, does not require hospitalization for a long time, is possible in outpatient settings and has a fairly loyal price – from $2500-$8000 per zone.

Some Ottawa clinics offer their clients liposuction for several zones and provide substantial discounts.

The method of liposuction involves suctioning excess fat accumulation with a vacuum pump.

The surgeon makes small incisions in the areas where the fat is located and introduces into these holes special cannulas connected to the pump.

Depending on the type of liposuction, general or local anesthesia can be used. Liposuction is known and under the name as linoplasty, which allows to improve the contours of the body.

With the problem of a disproportionate body shape, not only people with excess weight, but also thin women, who have uneven distribution of fat due to hereditary factors and individual characteristics of the body, can face.

Lipoplasty can improve the contours by removing fat deposits from undesirable areas. Even if after the procedure, if non-adherence to the recommended diet, fat accumulates, it will be distributed evenly and the silhouette will not become spoiled.

Liposuction allows you to remove excess fat on the thighs, neck, arms, abdomen, back, waist, cheeks, chest, calves, chin, ankles and so on. Liposuction can act as a concomitant or self-sufficient procedure.

Despite such a wide range of uses, liposuction will not have an effect with sheer obesity as an alternative to exercise and diet.

The procedure for liposuction involves several basic steps.

  • Inspection of the patient. The doctor examines the problem areas and the whole body and gives his approval for the procedure.
    Anesthesia. The anesthesiologist makes an injection to a patient with local or general anesthesia.
  • Incision. The surgeon treats the operating field, covers the areas with sterile bandages, treats the skin with a sterile solution to reduce the risk of bleeding, makes small incisions, usually up to 5 millimeters.
  • Sucking fat. Through thin tubes-cannulas, placed in the incisions, fat is pumped out. The removal of fat is accompanied by forward-backward movements. A special syringe is attached to the cannula, into which the removed fat is placed.
  • Recovery period. After the procedure, you can leave the clinic on the same day or the next – depending on the doctor’s recommendations. It is necessary to wear elastic bandages or compression underwear, which allows to control swelling and compress the skin, so that later it does not hang, repeating the contours of the body. In some cases, to remove excess fluid and blood, the tubes can be placed under the skin in the incisions made.

If this procedure is optional for the patient, the incisions are sewn immediately after the procedure.

Result. The first results from the procedure will be visible after 1.5 months. A tangible and noticeable result will be visible in 4-6 months after the procedure.

Indications and contraindications to the procedure of liposuction in Ottawa

Such an operation as liposuction is not recommended for all patients, as it will not always bring the desired result. Preference is given to patients not older than 40 years old, with good elasticity of the skin and local fatty deposits.

In addition, the indication for surgery is the absence of any pathologies. Liposuction is also designed for people who want to fight with cellulite – in this regard, such an operation will be very effective.

Before the procedure, it is necessary to take tests, after which an experienced competent surgeon will offer the patient a computer simulation of the results of liposuction, allowing you to see the future silhouette. If the patient remains satisfied, liposuction is prescribed.

In addition to indications, there are prohibitions that should not be violated. Here they are:

  • Posterior and anterior surfaces of lower leg
  • Front of thighs
  • Surfaces of forearms.

In addition to the above, one should take into account that liposuction does not eliminate such a problem as general obesity and is not recommended for pregnant women.

Refuse the procedure should be and people with diabetes, as well as with vascular and heart disease, as well as with the use of medicines, focused on healing wounds, treatment of thrombophlebitis and getting rid of edema.

What are the risks and complications associated with my procedure?

Small complications of the liposuction procedure should be considered necrosis, allergic reaction, scars, fainting and pigmentation. Possible reactions such as the manifestation of arrhythmias, heart attacks, infections and injuries of internal organs.

These reactions are extremely rare and occur with inexperienced medical attention. Unforeseen complications are possible, for example, ultrasound burn, swelling or hyperesthesia.
