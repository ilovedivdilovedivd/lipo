Liposuction technique

If we consider the technique of performing this procedure, then it is a suction of fatty fibers, this is done by applying a vacuum pump, in other words, using a “fatty vacuum cleaner”.

This manipulation is performed under general anesthesia.

In the subcutaneous fat layer, a thin tube is inserted through which the surgeon gently removes fat.

Philippines liposuction from other procedures is different: a great painstaking work of a surgeon, requiring increased attention, although technically the operation is not difficult.

This operation is highly effective and leads to a lasting result. If the patient gain weight after liposuction, has been carried out, the treated areas remain unchanged. After the procedure, there is almost no scarring.

Types of adipose tissue

In place of localization and features of metabolism, fatty fiber is divided into three types:

  1. Subcutaneous fat, consisting of fat.
  2. Deep fat layer.
  3. Inner fat, located in the abdominal cavity.

These three layers greatly affect the body of a person.

The distribution and location of the fat mass is determined by many parameters, but the main ones are:

  • Genetic predisposition.
  • Culture of nutrition.
  • Intensity of metabolism.
  • Sex.
  • Age.
  • Regularity of physical activity.

Each type of adipose tissue has its own peculiarities and patterns:

The fat layer under the skin is distributed throughout the body. The human roundness of outlines is determined by its thickness.

What determines the thickness of subcutaneous fat? From the culture of nutrition. As a rule, this layer easily comes to naught if you follow a diet or with the help of physical exercises.

The deep fat layer is distributed in the body not as evenly as the previous type. It is localized in some anatomical areas to a greater or lesser degree and determines the individuality of the bpdy, for example, it can be present on the abdomen, sides, hips.

The fat cells, which are deep, are distinguished by a special tissue metabolism. They are localized in a specific place in a certain volume and have a genetic code, so they change slightly, even with a lot of weight loss.

The internal fat is contained in the abdominal cavity, it envelops internal organs and retroperitoneal space. The fat layer under the skin can have a normal thickness, so to reduce the amount of internal fat, one should observe the energy adequacy in nutrition. Types and forms of fat

The volume of tissue increases due to the hypertrophy of cells. This process can be launched in tissues of any kind and localization, so this leads to the formation of two types of fat concentrations: local and general. The local type is due to the increased sensitivity of fat cells to the incoming glucose.

This property, in most cases, is due to a hereditary factor. All types of local fat concentrations distinguish stability in maintaining shape and quantity even with a large weight loss.

There are three forms of local hypertrophy of fatty fibers:

  1. Diffusely-local.
  2. Fat traps.
  3. Contour violations of small-hummocky character.

When diffusely local form hypertrophy fat cells formed in the surface layer of adipose tissue, focusing in certain areas of the body.

In most cases it is the front of the hips, the upper part of the abdomen, the lower leg. These fat concentrations without clear boundaries, which causes their smooth transition into an area with a normal thickness of subcutaneous fat.

Fat traps are fat deposits with more or less distinct boundaries with a sharp deformation of the silhouette contours in a limited area of the body.

The reason lies in the hypertrophy of the deep fat layer. These fat traps can be observed on the outer surface of the thighs. Often they are called “ears on the hips.”

Finely uneven contour abnormalities may appear as in the case of localized deposits form, and in general (obesity). Small-bumpy formations appear as a result of a special metabolism of fat cells located in the subdermal layer, and also because of the anatomical and physiological features of the structure of the fat layer on the surface.

For example, we found out that these cells have an increased sensitivity to glucose, which enters the body, so under it, they begin to increase in size.

In the subdermal layer, the connective bridges do not have sufficient elasticity, so when the adipocytes increase, they do not have the ability to stretch, which is reflected on the surface of the skin that swells and resembles an “orange peel”.

This phenomenon is called cellulite. However, this definition is not entirely correct and means “inflammation of the subcutaneous tissue”. If to speak the medical term, then “small-bumpy lipodystrophy” will be more correct.

General appearance (obesity) appears in the absence of a culture of food intake, simply speaking – if energy adequacy in nutrition is not observed. This is the main trigger for the gradual weight gain.

During this process, changes in fatty tissues occur both in the surface and in the deep layer. With obesity, the thickness of subcutaneous fat can reach several centimeters, and the boundaries of “fat traps” cease to be clearly defined.

These changes often occur in adulthood and require conservative treatment, which should lead to a balance of the amount of incoming energy and its consumption.

In this case, liposuction can be considered as an additional method of treatment.
