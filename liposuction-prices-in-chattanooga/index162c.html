Liposuction Prices In Chattanooga

Liposuction in Chattanooga is a cosmetic operation, during which the surplus of adipose tissue is removed from a specific area of the human body.

Liposuction is used to destroy fat, which is not obtained by dieting or exercise.

Usually it is necessary to make the body more proportionate, improve its appearance and adjust the shape.

In rare cases, liposuction is used for severe obesity, when it is necessary to reduce the fat volume as soon as possible and by any methods, because excess weight creates a threat to the life of the patient. Also, liposuction removes the lipomas.

Can Liposuction Help You Lose Weight?

This is not quite true. Liposuction helps to get rid of excess fat in certain parts of the body. As a rule, patients choose hips, knees, stomach, chest, hands, chin area. But this is a local effect, which as a whole will not change anything in the body. After one operation, you can not immediately lose weight everywhere and turn into a slender person.

Typically, it makes sense to do liposuction, if you already lost weight, have learned to maintain the weight you need, but you are not satisfied with the fat accumulated in one place, which refuses to disappear, despite your efforts. Liposuction in Chattanooga TN does not help cure obesity, get rid of cellulite or stretch marks.

How many pounds can I lose after the operation?

As a rule, no more than 2.5 liters of fat is pumped out in one operation. That is, global weight loss will not.

How is surgery performed?

There are several techniques that differ slightly from each other. The general principle is to insert into the body of a tube, by means of which excess fat can be pumped out from under the skin.

First, the patient is prepared for an operation that is performed under anesthesia (sometimes they can use epidural anesthesia if fat is supposed to be pumped out from the lower half of the body). Then the area where liposuction will be performed is treated with special medications.

Then you need to prepare fat tissue: break the cells with ultrasound, a laser or the introduction of a special solution. The broken fat with the help of tubes is sucked from the body.

After the operation, remove the tubes, apply seams and put on the patient bandages and special underwear. The operation lasts 1-3 hours, depending on the extent of fat removal.

Which method is better?

The most gentle technique is tumescent liposuction. This is a procedure for which there may be enough local anesthesia. In the area where the operation is performed, a lot of medicines are injected: lidocaine, vasoconstrictor and saline solution, which helps to remove fat. For small areas on the face, laser liposuction is often used: the tubes for removing fat are smaller in diameter than with other methods.

What should I do before liposuction?

To begin with, make sure that you need this operation. If you are obese, liposuction will not help – you need to bring the weight back to normal with non-surgical methods.

Then you need to understand that you can keep your weight in a normal framework, that is, spend at least six months in a stable weight.

If you still want to have surgery, it makes sense to prepare mentally, perhaps even to visit a psychologist. After all, surgery is a complex procedure that only removes part of the fat, but does not change the rest of life and does not help to become more active and better.

After that, you need to choose a clinic and follow all the surgeon’s recommendations, buy compression underwear and tune in for a recovery period.

How long does it take to recover?

It depends on the method of operation that you choose. Some traumatize more, some less. After some methods, recovery takes up to three months. More modern operations require two weeks of recovery, but you can return to work in a few days (if the work does not involve large physical exertion).

After the operation, you will need to wear special underwear and bandages, so that there are no swelling and no hematomas formed. Also, antibiotics can be prescribed for the prevention of infections, as well as pain medications, while the damaged tissues heal.

Several days after the operation, you can not drive, for the entire period of healing you need to forget about bad habits. The doctor will tell you more about how long it will take to give up workouts and exercises.

Is it true? Can fat return after liposuction?

It’s possible. In a single session, a small amount of fat is deliberately removed so that the effect of a fast diet does not appear when the body is gaining the usual fat supply at a high rate. In the rest, much depends on individual characteristics: the tendency to excess weight, compliance with diet and physical activity. In any case, it is customary to talk about a good effect of the operation not earlier than six months after the intervention, although the results are noticeable immediately after the inevitable edema disappears.

Liposuction contraindicated for people who has:

  1. Diabetes.
  2. Diseases of blood, disorders of clotting.
  3. Oncological diseases.
  4. Diseases of the kidneys and liver.
  5. Thrombophlebitis, varicose veins.
  6. Diseases of the cardiovascular system.

What are the complications and side effects of liposuction?

The risk of unpleasant consequences is, as with any other surgical intervention. It depends on many factors: beginning with the skill of surgeons and ending with the characteristics of the patient’s body.

The most unpleasant consequences are:

  1. Blockage of blood vessels by thrombus or fat.
  2. Hematomas and swelling. Most are absorbed in a few weeks or months.
  3. Inflammation.
  4. Numbness in the area of liposuction, which lasts for several months.
  5. Change in skin color in the area of operation.

The effect of corrugation. With uneven pumping of fat, cones and irregularities are formed, so that the body area after liposuction resembles a washing board.

Asymmetry. If the operation fails, the fat tissue is distributed unevenly and the disproportions of the body are visible.

Does Liposuction Leave Scars?

From the operation there are scars in places where tubes were injected under the skin. Scars can be almost invisible.

Should I Get Liposuction Done?

Only you can answer this question. Liposuction is, as a rule, a cosmetic procedure.

If you are not going to lose weight with it, become healthier, improve your personal life or solve all problems in a moment, but you are sure that the operation will help you feel better – consult with specialists and make a decision.
