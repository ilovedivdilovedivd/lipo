Also known as lipomodeling, liposuction in Manhattan is the removal of excess body fat, improving the contours of your body, changing the proportions and ultimately enhancing your attractiveness.

Despite good health and a sufficient level of physical activity, some people can still have a body with disproportionate contours due to congenital features of the structure, excess weight or localized fatty deposits.

In some cases, liposuction is used in conjunction with other procedures, such as contouring, facelift, breast reduction, breast or abdominal lift.

Consultation before liposuction in Manhattan

The success and safety of liposuction in many ways depends on your frankness during the preliminary consultation. Your plastic surgeon will ask you a few questions about your health, wishes and lifestyle.

Be ready to discuss at the consultation:

  • Why do you want to do liposuction?
  • What result do you wish to receive upon completion?
  • What are the diseases you have had in recent times?
  • Do you have any allergies to medications or another form of allergy?
  • What medicines or dietary supplements do you currently take?
  • Do you drink alcohol or smoke?
  • Which previous operations did you transfer?

How is the operation performed?

Step 1 – Anesthesia

Anesthetic medications are introduced to ensure your complete comfort during the procedure. Your doctor will select them individually for you.

Step 2 – incision

Liposuction is carried out through small, almost inconspicuous incisions, which then completely disappear. First, a special sterile solution is introduced through these notches to disinfect and reduce bleeding and trauma. Then, with the help of thin hollow tubes, the required amount of fat is weakened, displaced and removed from the treatment area.

Step 3 – View Results

The results of liposuction in Manhattan will be visible when subside little postoperative swelling and fluid leaves, usually linger in the place of impact on your body.

AFTER LIPOSUCTION

After the procedure is carried out, it is necessary to wear some compression or elastic bandages for a while. They will help reduce swelling after surgery and tighten the skin on the new contours of your body. You will receive specific instructions to expedite your recovery, which may include:

  1. How to take care of an operated area of your body.
  2. What medications can be used to accelerate healing.
  3. How to reduce the likelihood of infection.

Especially it should be noted: sometimes after liposuction you may be recommended to reduce excess skin. Your doctor will suggest this in advance.

How much does liposuction cost?

Before planning the surgery, the patient and the doctor discuss how much liposuction will cost. Prices for liposuction can vary widely. Typically, this depends on the amount of work and complexity of the site, but other factors, such as changes in the price of drugs, materials may interfere.

The doctor calculates how much liposuction costs, based on the following factors:

  • Volume of fat removed;
  • complexity of operated areas;
  • method of liposuction;
  • the type of anesthesia and the drugs used;
  • The final cost is specified on consultation with our surgeon.
