Liposuction In Lakeland FL

To remove excessive accumulation of fatty deposits in certain areas of the human body, liposuction is used. A factor that significantly affects the success of the procedure is the elasticity of the skin.

The younger the patient, the correspondingly, the more elastic his skin, which can quickly return the form, that is, the operation will be more effective. When performing liposuction in elderly patients, there is a risk of skin roughness.

How does rehabilitation after liposuction last?

Depending on the type of operation, recovery can last from ten days to six months. Even in the first days after liposuction in Lakeland, the patient does not experience severe pain.

The only reason for them to stay for about a day in the clinic is to control the fluid, which for some time comes out of the places where punctures were made. About a week a person needs to limit all kinds of physical activity, so that the body can recover, after a surgical procedure.

To reduce puffiness and accelerate the process of recovery of subcutaneous tissues, it is recommended to wear special compression clothes around the clock for about a month.

And after this period, another two or three weeks, the pulling bandage can be worn only during the day, and at night it can be removed. The type of compression bandage directly depends on the site being operated. Therefore, before going to surgery, it is very important to take care of buying a suitable compression bandage.

The first two or three days can be expected that the swelling will increase. Swelling should persist for up to 5 days. Then it must begin to decrease.
After about three months, the result will be seen.

It is necessary to be adjusted, that the certain time on a body essential bruises will be appreciable. First external, and then those that formed in the deeper layers of subcutaneous tissue.

Over time, hematomas heal naturally. Since small holes through which the fat mass is sucked out, it is not necessary to remove the seams by sewing with self-absorbing threads.

During recovery period, it is forbidden to overheat the body. This means you can not visit the sauna or solarium. In no case you can not sunbathe or swim in the sea, in the river or in the pool.

Even a hot bath is not very desirable for a person during this period. The main struggle is aimed at combating puffiness. And in order to keep it, you must avoid physical exertion and in no case overheat.

Also, a normal reaction of the body is considered if the patient for a period of up to six months feels numbness in the skin. After liposuction, it is very useful to do lymph drainage massage, aimed at stimulating the movement of lymph along the body. Massage helps a person recover faster, and his skin becomes more elastic and returns to his normal state.

What are the possible complications?

After liposuction in Lakeland, there is a risk of complications is quite high. Therefore it is very important to obey the doctor’s recommendation.

This will help prevent unwanted consequences. In addition, it is very important in the course of rehabilitation to immediately consult your doctor if you notice that the first signs of early complications appeared. These include:

Hematomas are small bruises after surgery should not cause the patient to worry, since this is a normal process of recovery after liposuction. But if a person notices too large hematomas, he should consult a doctor to do blood aspiration. Otherwise, there may be inflammation or suppuration, passing into the fistula and even into blood infection.

Seroma or an accumulation under the skin of the lymphatic fluid appears already on the second third day after liposuction. The appearance of a complication may be due to the fact that too much solution has been injected or the lymphatic duct has been damaged during the operation. To remove seroma, it is necessary to perform lymph drainage. This procedure may take several days.

Necrosis is an inflammation of the subcutaneous tissue caused by infection. At the first sign of inflammation it is very important to undergo a full course of treatment with the use of antibiotics.

Hyperpigmentation or spots on the skin most often occurs due to the fact that a person during recovery period is in the sun. It happens that the hyperpigmentation arises because of the individual characteristics of the organism. Hyperesthesia or hypersensitivity in the area of liposuction. To remove the persistent pain you need to see a doctor.

The more recent complications after liposuction are:

Irregularities on the body. The problem can appear for two reasons. Either the surgeon unevenly perform fat aspiration, or the patient does not follow the recommendations regarding wearing compression underwear or under physical stress. Irregularities, depending on their degree can make a human body shape disproportionately.

Changing the body shape. If a person does not change his dietary style and fat cells continue to grow, then this process will occur more intensively in those places where liposuction was not done. Therefore, the body can take the most unexpected form. To avoid this complication, it is very important to change the eating style.

Sagging of the skin is a complication, appears due to the fact, that the skin is not elastic enough. Most often this problem appears after liposuction of the chin. Get rid of excess skin, you need to perform a skin tightening.
