Tijuana Liposuction Prices And Photos

Liposuction in Tijuana is a surgical operation that allows you to quickly and effectively remove subcutaneous fat deposits, adjust the contours of the body, perform lipomodeling, make the body more harmonious and attractive.

Liposuction today is one of the most popular plastic surgeries in the US and around the world, because the characteristics of the structure of the female body cause the accumulation of local fat deposits, which are difficult to combat even with the help of diets and painstaking training.

It should be noted that liposuction is primarily an aesthetic procedure, so medical indications for its conduct are not needed.

But you can not treat liposuction as a way to treat obesity.

Liposuction does not eliminate the causes of fat formation, which can be both hormonal failures, and improper metabolism, and an imbalance between diet and physical activity. Liposuction is just a correction of the consequences of these disorders.

Important features of liposuction are that this operation is characterized by high efficiency and stability of the result: even in case of weight loss after surgery, the correction zones remain practically unchanged.

In addition, liposuction almost does not leave scarring.

All methods of removing subcutaneous fat can be divided into three groups:

  • Invasive (surgical) – surgical removal and redistribution of fat deposits (lipomodeling) in certain areas of the body;
  • Non-invasive – splitting of fat with its subsequent excretion through the circulatory and lymphatic systems;
  • Minimally invasive – the effect on fat cells, their destruction and excretion through micro-punctures.

Surgical (invasive) liposuction is of three types:

  • Classical vacuum dry liposuction;
  • Classical vacuum wet liposuction;
  • Tumescent liposuction.

With liposuction in Tijuana, fat is removed through small incisions with an aspirating (suction) device and a special cannula tube. The above mentioned methods differ among themselves in the way of pretreatment of the corrected zone, the type of anesthesia, the size of the cannula, the permissible volume of pumped out fat, the duration of the recovery period.

The oldest technique is classical dry liposuction, patients’ feedback about which leaves much to be desired due to the large trauma and high blood loss. The procedure is performed under general anesthesia.

First, a solution that helps dissolve the fat cells is injected subcutaneously into the correction area. After that, through small (up to 0.5 mm) incisions with a cannula tube and a vacuum pump, aspiration (suction) of fat up to 0.39 gallon is carried out.

Classical moist liposuction is a more gentle procedure. In the aspiration area, a solution with an anesthetic is injected, which facilitates the destruction of fat cells, after which it is pumped to 1.32 gallon of fat. At small volumes lipomodeling can be performed under local anesthesia.

Tumescent liposuction, according to patient feedback, is considered one of the most popular procedures. It differs from the previous two by a special formula of the solution, invented by the Californian dermatologist Klein. Due to the special composition of the preparation before the aspiration procedure, the fat is converted into a liquid emulsion and can be removed in a volume of up to 2.64-3.17 gallon during surgery as safely as possible via a vacuum method through a thin cannula. There is enough local anesthesia when processing 1-2 zones.

Non-invasive methods of fat removal include ultrasonic (cavitation), radio-frequency and chemical methods, to minimally invasive methods – laser. These modern techniques use special equipment that destroys the fat. They do not provide for surgical intervention, so liposuction is difficult to name.

Hardware techniques are usually used for minor correction with liposuction of the face, knees, forearms. To expect from them the cardinal results that classical or tumescent liposuction of the chest, abdomen, thighs can provide is not worth it.

Ultrasonic liposuction is carried out with the help of a special device that destroys adipose tissue due to generated ultrasonic pulses.

This kind of liposuction is similar to the tumescent technique: first, infiltration of subcutaneous fat with Klein solution is carried out, and then by means of a cannula with an ultrasound dissector at the end the liposuction is performed.

Laser liposuction provides the result due to the laser action on adipose tissue: the cell membrane of fat cells is destroyed, tissues warm up, bleeding decreases and collagen production is stimulated.

Water-jet liposuction is performed using an apparatus that supplies water under pressure to the subcutaneous tissue with a special cannula. The effect is on the adipose tissue, while the blood vessels and nerves are not injured.

Radio wave or radiofrequency liposuction is a successful attempt to improve liposuction methods. Processing of tissues is due to radio wave radiation. To date, liposuction can be performed by any of the above methods: both operating and hardware.

The choice of method in each individual case depends on the indications, on the expected results, and also on the characteristics of the organism. The procedure is determined during a consultation by a plastic surgeon and is confirmed by the written consent of the patient.

Preparation for the procedure

Preparation for liposuction begins with the consultation of a plastic surgeon. The doctor will conduct an examination, determine the scope of the intervention and propose a procedure, and then prescribe a preliminary examination.

Usually before liposuction it is required to pass a blood test, an electrocardiogram, and also to receive consultation of the therapist, endocrinologist, gynecologist.

Based on the results of the survey, the surgeon at the final consultation determines the method of liposuction, gives recommendations for the preparation, informs the patient about some details of the operation and the characteristics of the recovery period.
