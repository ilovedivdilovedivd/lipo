Liposuction Knoxville TN Prices And Before & After Photos

Liposuction in Knoxville is a kind of plastic surgery, the purpose of which is to improve the contours of the face or body by removing excess fat in certain areas.

Most often, patients undergo such surgery in the abdomen, thighs and buttocks, but in many cases this manipulation is combined with other operations on the face, chest and other areas of the body.

Liposuction is carried out zonally, in places of excessive accumulation of fatty tissue, and can not be carried out in order to get rid of obesity.

It is also impossible to get rid of the impending “fat apron” of the anterior abdominal wall with the help of liposuction.

To solve this problem, it is recommended to perform abdominoplasty. An important condition for successful liposuction is the degree of elasticity of your skin. Contraindications are: diabetes, bleeding disorders.

What is liposuction performed?

Liposuction is performed under general anesthesia. The duration of the operation depends on the volume determined by the surgeon in a preliminary consultation with the patient.

After the operation, the patient has to stay one day in the hospital and then wear a special compression underwear for a month.

Seams are removed one week after the operation.

In the postoperative period, the patient has swelling of soft tissues, hematomas, which persist for up to three weeks.

The final result of the operation can be assessed after three months, but the first noticeable changes in the contours become visible at the end of two weeks after the liposuction.

Types of liposuction in Knoxville

Mechanical liposuction. Removal of fat by long curettes due to negative pressure in vacuum suction.

Tumescent liposuction. Injection with Klein solution in the operation area, which contributes to the maximum reduction of blood vessels and nerve endings.Emulsified fatty tissue is removed by thin cannulas.

Ultrasonic liposuction is aimed at destroying fat cells by ultrasound, turning fat into an emulsion, which is removed by cannulae using vacuum suction.
Water jet liposuction. A fan-shaped jet of water under pressure produces the separation of loose fat cells from the elastic connective tissue.

Electrolipolysis is the destruction of fat cells by discharges of electric current, the conversion of fat into emulsion, which is removed by cannulas with the help of vacuum suction.

Ultrasonic liposuction in Knoxville

Many people are eager to find non-surgical techniques for liposuction, and this is understandable. However, as a result of numerous advertising promises of “non-surgical ultrasound liposuction”, “non-invasive liposuction”, there is some confusion.

So, ultrasonic liposuction is the destruction of fat cells by ultrasound, followed by their removal by a traditional vacuum method through the cannula or with the withdrawal through the venous or lymphatic system (a new and controversial method).

The method of conventional ultrasound liposuction differs from other conventional techniques only in the way that fat is converted into an emulsion.

This technique has a number of advantages and a number of shortcomings, which the surgeon will best describe to you on a personal consultation. It allows you to remove a large enough amount of fat in one session without significant blood loss, removing fat deposits.

But ultrasound can also have an adverse effect on subcutaneous fatty tissue, which, in turn, can lead to uneven skin or the appearance of scar deformities or seroma. That is why any method of liposuction should be selected by a professional surgeon specifically for you.

Benefits of liposuction

The advantages undoubtedly include that it is a low-traumatic surgical intervention. From the point of view of aesthetics, it is important that liposuction is performed through minimal incisions (up to 0.5 cm), and there is no trace of the operation with time.

In addition, unlike other plastic surgeries, the operation can be performed immediately in several zones, and, if necessary, repeated after a short period of time. If, in the future, you gain weight, the new fat deposits will be distributed evenly, and will not deform the new contours of your body.
