Preparing for liposuction and the process.

The entire procedure for removing the fat layer by the presented method entails the following actions in the sequence:

The preparatory stage for liposuction is a consultation with a surgeon, which determines the amount of fat removal and offers options for intervention.

Then a preliminary examination is carried out: blood test, ECG, the conclusion of the therapist and endocrinologist, for women – also the gynecologist.

According to the analysis, the doctor determines the type of liposuction and gives advice on how to prepare for the procedure.

The procedure begins with preparation for pumping out adipose tissue – the introduction of anesthesia, the use of local anesthesia.

Then the doctor makes cuts in the planned zones, then to introduce through them cannulas to which the vacuum pump joins and the emulsion is removed – the fat tissue has already turned into it.

If no more than 6 lb of fat is removed, then mechanical effects are applied. With the expected pumping of large volumes, the zone is processed ultrasound or laser at first.

Important! After liposuction, pain discomfort, swelling, and fever may occur. A day later, a dressing is done. You can take a shower only after a week, but compression underwear should be worn at least 4-6 weeks.

The final result is clearly visible after 2 months. For the period of recovery should limit physical activity, refrain from visiting the sauna and solarium.

Liposuction is a serious procedure, and not just an exercise leading to weight loss. To treat it stands with understanding and with all responsibility.

Accurate performance of all appointments and recommendations of the physician allows for a qualitative and effective process of fat removal.

Liposuction has some features that every client should know about:

  • Pumping fat does not leave any traces on the skin from the operation, there may be barely noticeable scars.
  • The procedure is carried over easily and with all the recommendations of the surgeon, the result is kept for a long time.
  • Liposuction is performed as an independent procedure, or together with other aesthetic operations. The process of fat removal can take place in several problem areas simultaneously.

This is interesting: The duration of the operation depends on the number of treated zones and the volume of pumped fat, which can last from 1 to 3 hours. The available data and indications directly affect the cost of surgical intervention.

Can I ask for help from a plastic surgeon?

Perhaps, only happy owners of an impeccable figure, and also very thin by nature people can confidently state that they will never have to resort to liposuction.

The others, having meticulously examined themselves in the mirror, easily name one or several problem areas, which may one day become the object of surgical intervention.

Here is a portrait of a candidate for liposuction (based on the data of surgeons):

  1. a person over 18 years of age;
  2. weight normal or excessive (but body mass index – less than 30);
  3. there are no serious chronic diseases;
  4. Skin firmness and elasticity;
  5. on the body there are areas with fatty deposits that do not “go away” even after a diet or long-term sports training.

Liposuction is contraindicated in the following cases:

  • in the presence of chronic systemic diseases (severe hypertension, diabetes, kidney failure);
  • with problems with blood coagulability, a tendency to thrombosis;
  • with skin changes in the area of exposure (dermatological diseases, extensive scars after surgery or burns);
  • with obesity;
  • at infectious diseases – in acute or chronic stage;
  • with mental disorders;
  • with allergies to drugs used during the operation.
