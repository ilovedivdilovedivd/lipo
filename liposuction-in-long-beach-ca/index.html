Liposuction In Long Beach CA: Prices And Before And After Photos

Each liposuction procedure begins with a consultation. The doctor will evaluate your health, both physical and psychological, and determine if you are a good candidate for this procedure, based on the elasticity of the skin and the places of fat deposits.

Then it will help you decide which liposuction procedure is most suitable for you. Most likely, laboratory tests will be required to confirm the state of health.

This examination will include a complete health history and, most likely, a detailed history of weight gain.

You may also be required to stop or start taking certain medications, for example, avoid taking aspirin, many anti-inflammatory drugs and herbal supplements 2 weeks before surgery.

Before the procedure, the doctor notes from which area (one or more) the fat will be removed.

Anesthesia for liposuction in Long Beach

Like most types of surgical procedures, liposuction requires anesthesia. The type of anesthesia depends on where and in what area the liposuction is performed.

For small areas, doctors can use local anesthesia, which will freeze only the area involved in the operation. Typically, the doctor enters sedatives together with local anesthesia to relax the patient.

Sometimes, the doctor can give the patient an epidural anesthesia that is injected intravenously and blocks the sensations of the whole body (for example, from the waist down).

General anesthesia can be used in a hospital setting when a large amount of fat is to be removed. In any case, your doctor will recommend for you the best choice of anesthesia.

Liposuction incisions

Typically, with liposuction, the incisions are very small (about 0.3 – 1.5 cm each) and your surgeon will take care to place cuts on the body where they will not be visible.

Often when treating large areas, several incisios are made for each treated area. Liposuction, course of operation

During the operation, the doctor inserts the cannula through the incision into the fatty layers of the designated areas. The cannula quickly moves back and forth to release the fat cells, which are aspirated, usually with a vacuum device connected to the cannula, and poured into a special flask.

Cannula creates tunnels in the fat layer to create a new body shape. For this reason, the patient should wear compression underwear after surgery. Because patients also lose fluid during the procedure, intravenous fluid is sometimes required after surgery.

The incisions are closed with surgical sutures. In some cases, drainage tubes may be installed in the incision locations to help drain excess liquid.

Recovery after liposuction in Long Beach

If the patient is under local anesthesia, he can usually go home the day after the operation. General anesthesia often requires one night in a hospital or surgical center. After the procedure, many patients have drainage tubes for drainage for 2-5 days.

Often patients are advised to wear compression underwear to compress the operated area to reduce swelling and promote early healing. Antibiotics can also be prescribed to prevent infection. Most patients can return to lax work after only 2 to 3 days.

Hard work can be resumed, at least not earlier than 2 to 3 weeks after liposuction in Long Beach, depending on which areas have been treated. As with any surgery, it is important to understand that these conditions can vary widely depending on the physical condition of the patient, the methods used, and other concomitant operating conditions.

Despite this, it is important to take care not to receive excessive force, avoid friction at the site of the procedure, or sudden movements during the healing period.

The stitches at the site of the incision are removed or dissolved after about 10 days. Many patients experience pain, tenderness, or burning during the healing process, but these symptoms usually disappear within three weeks. After about four to six weeks, the swelling should come off, and you will be able to see the results achieved.

Patients should also avoid heavy physical exercise and other physical exertion within a month after the procedure.
