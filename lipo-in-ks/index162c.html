Lipo In Kansas

Liposuction in Kansas is an effective surgical method of the body correction. The correction of the body should be understood as the elimination of local fat deposits, which lead to the so-called lipo disproportion.

Lipoaspiration in aesthetic surgery can be claimed for a more visible relationship for the following reasons:

– it is one of the most common operations in plastic surgery;
– Technically operational methodology is relatively simple;
– as a result, the patient receives a fairly persistent and noticeable result;

With the right approach, liposuction in Kansas is one of the safest operations, after which the percentage of complications is minimal.

With the use of modern techniques, surgery is low-traumatic and has a short rehabilitation period. In fact, liposuction is no more dangerous than all other plastic surgeries.

Of course, one can constantly do various cosmetic procedures and hopes that these “breeches” will be lost. You can actually perform liposuction on any part of the body.

In general, excess local fat deposits are localized in the abdominal region, the outer contour of the thighs, the inner surface of the thigh, flanks, waist region, the region of the knee joints, the “second” chin.

Anesthesia

Type of anesthesia is discussed in the consultation with the doctor. The choice will largely be determined by the alleged scope of intervention and the psychological portrait of the patient. The amount of local anesthetic that the doctor can use is simultaneously limited to a sufficiently strict safe dose range of the drug.

Therefore, if liposuction in Kansas is supposed to be from 2 to 4 small areas, then it can be performed under local anesthesia. With lipocorrection of more than 4 areas, preference should be given to intravenous anesthesia. Also, remember that some people are allergic to preperat used for local anesthesia, then in this case the procedure should be carried out under general anesthesia.

The psychological component plays a significant role in the choice of anesthesia. Patients with increased emotional lability tend to be much less tolerant of local anesthesia, so these people also prefer to use general anesthesia.

However, I want to emphasize that this issue is solved with the doctor individually for each patient taking into account all factors.

Contraindications

Contraindications may be local and general. Local: the presence of inflammatory diseases on the skin and subcutaneous fat in the area of liposuction.

General contraindications are: arterial hypertension with a history of hypertensive crises; insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus; Iron-deficiency anemia;
thrombophlebitis of deep veins of lower extremities; bronchial asthma without long-term remission; thyrotoxic goiter; ischemic heart disease (postinfarction cardiosclerosis); atrial fibrillation; acute infectious and respiratory diseases.

Preparing for surgery

Before the operation it is necessary to make a series of laboratory tests appointed by the doctor and, if necessary, to have a consultation with an ophthalmologist.

At least a week before the operation, you should stop taking aspirin and its derivatives, exclude alcohol within 2-3 days, stop smoking within a few days.

Smoking reduces the intensity of blood flow and thereby slows down the reparative processes in the postoperative area.

Before the operation it is desirable to have a good sleep and rest – this will help to avoid unnecessary nervous tension in connection with the operation.

The cost

The price of the liposuction operation varies depending on the volume of the proposed surgical intervention, the type of anesthesia, and also the volume of preoperative examination. You can find out the final price of the operation after a meeting with the doctor at an initial consultation.
