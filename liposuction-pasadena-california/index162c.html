Types of liposuction in Pasadena

Liposuction is a modeling of the contours of the body, literally – the removal of excess fatty tissue, which accumulates in problem areas.

The problem areas include the abdomen, waist, back, hips, buttocks, the inner surface of the hands and knees. Local fat deposits, unfortunately, live their own lives and can not be corrected by exercise or diet. The only way to get rid of them remains liposuction.

There are many types of liposuction, each of which has its own characteristics. All of them differ in the mechanism of action on fat cells.

At the beginning of the technique, mechanical liposuction was used – a very traumatic procedure with unpredictable consequences.

It was replaced by ultrasonic liposuction, which has undergone significant changes over the past 10 years. And today plastic surgeons resort to this type of liposuction, demonstrating stable aesthetic results of body contour correction.

To more modern kinds of liposuction it is possible to carry a laser liposuction. The effect on fat cells is carried out with the help of a laser, which literally “evaporates” them. Laser liposuction has a slight lifting effect, but the possibilities of radiofrequency liposuction are still far from the laser liposuction.

Radiofrequency liposuction is the ability to remove excess fat and simultaneously tighten the skin. The need for this is dictated by the fact, that when performing any other liposuction there is a risk of sagging the skin.

If the patient has a large amount of fat, if the skin is not elastic enough, then there is a risk that, after removing the fat, the skin will not contract and lead to the effect of the “washing board”.

RF liposuction due to controlled thermal action stimulates the process of skin contraction, the results of which after correction, the relief is smooth and the skin is smooth.

Another type of liposuction in Pasadena, which has earned the respect of specialists and the trust of patients, is water-jet liposuction. Simulation of the contours of the body during water-jet liposuction is due to the gentle “washing away” of the solution of the fat cells by the jet.

The remaining vessels and cells are not damaged in this case. Water jet liposuction, as well as radiofrequency liposuction, are considered low-traumatic techniques that are comfortably tolerated by patients and do not involve complex long-term recovery.
