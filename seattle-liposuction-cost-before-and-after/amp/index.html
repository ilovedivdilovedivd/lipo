Seattle Liposuction Cost & Before And After

Liposuction in Seattle, WA is one of the most progressive methods of body correction, which allows you to quickly get rid of extra pounds and change your life forever.

Liposuction is designed to fight local fatty deposits (“fat traps”), which can not be removed with any non-surgical methods. The purpose of the procedure is not to fight against general obesity and not weight loss, but, first and foremost, correction of problem areas.

Contraindications to the removal of adipose tissue by this method are:

– Serious somatic diseases (pneumonia, hepatitis, tuberculosis, hypertension and heart disease, diabetes mellitus, etc.);
– oncological diseases;
– Mental disorders.

In addition, it is strictly forbidden to perform liposuction during pregnancy. In other cases, this is really an effective procedure that will allow you to take a huge step towards the perfect body.

Features of liposuction

Although today there are a lot of effective methods of fat burning, liposuction continues to cause a lot of controversy. This is explained by the “liposuction revolution” that occurred in the nineties.

Today, liposuction is one of the safest, fastest and most effective procedures. Damage to the body is now completely ruled out.

Any disputes about unpleasant consequences are absolutely groundless.

General scheme of plastic surgery

  • Preoperative marking.
  • Marking puncture sites.
  • Fluid injection into the marked areas.
  • Introduction of an ultrasound probe for atraumatic separation of fat from surrounding tissues.
  • Vacuum suction of the resulting finely divided fat.
  • Introduction of fat in other areas (if necessary).

Liposuction has a number of limitations. For example, for 1 procedure you can not aspirate (remove) more than 0.79-1.58 gallon of fat. The exact volume depends on the type of technique. The operation lasts from 1.5 to 2 hours.

Duration is determined by the amount of fat that is removed. Complex liposuction is performed under general anesthesia. Puncture after liposuction is not sutured for the outflow of the suppository. In a few days they are glued together.

Recovery period after liposuction

Liposuction, the photo “before” and “after” which amaze the imagination of all who, one way or another, faced the problem of fatty deposits, will be effective only if the postoperative period passes without complications.

It is important to remember that liposuction is a serious surgical operation, rehabilitation after which can take from 6 to 8 weeks.

In the first days after the procedure, you may experience certain inconveniences, such as, for example:

  • swelling and bruising (disappears within 1-2 weeks);
  • Fluid accumulation (eliminated by puncture);
  • temperature increase.

At this time it is important to be constantly supervised by experienced doctors and be able to consult with them if necessary.

The less fat was removed during the procedure, the less noticeable will be the effects of liposuction. Sutures are removed after 6-7 days after the procedure. In order to achieve the maximum effect of liposuction, on the recommendation of the doctor the patient should wear special compression clothes for a month, attend massages and physiotherapy sessions.

It is important to monitor your diet: despite the fact that liposuction is very effective, you should not abuse this achievement of scientific progress.

How fast will the result be visible?

Even modern liposuction is not omnipotent: do not expect a momentary “magic” effect. As practice shows, preliminary assessment can be made only 4-5 weeks after the procedure, and the final result will be visible only after 3-6 months. If the final result does not satisfy the patient, repeated liposuction is allowed.

What you can expect?

  • Hateful “fat traps” disappear instantly;
  • Simultaneous skin tightening (including in the most problematic places);
  • Perfect result of the operation, which will remain with you forever.
