Liposuction Sydney Prices

Liposuction in Sydney is an operation to remove excess fat deposits from various parts of the body under vacuum.

A small catheter is inserted through a small hole under the skin, through which excess fat is sucked off. It is possible to perform liposuction of the abdomen, thighs, sacral region, hands, as well as liposuction of the cheeks and liposuction of the chin.

The operation is performed both under local anesthesia and under general anesthesia. Recently, so-called ultrasonic liposuction, a non-traumatic and painless procedure, is becoming widespread.

The best results due to greater elasticity of tissues are usually observed in patients younger than 45 years of age.

In the our clinic, liposuction of the abdomen, shins and other areas of the body is performed using both classical and modern technologies.

This allows you to choose for each patient the best method of fat removal, shorten the length of the recovery period, minimize the likelihood of complications and make the results of the operation as appropriate to your expectations.

Depending on how much subcutaneous fat should be removed and in which part of the body, local anesthesia or general anesthesia can be used.

After processing the operating field with special solutions that disinfect the surface of the skin, the surgeon makes small punctures on it. They are placed miniature tubes, through which the removal of subcutaneous fat with a vacuum aspirator.

The duration of the operation is about 1 hour, after which the drainage tubes are placed in the punctures, and the patient is transferred to the ward under the supervision of the surgeon and anesthesiologist.

After 1-2 days, the tubes are removed, and the wound is sealed with a patch. Traces of interference completely disappear or become invisible to the naked eye.

Preparing for liposuction

Preparing for liposuction in Sydney is an indispensable step, that minimizes any, even improbable, risk to the patient’s health. It begins already at the first consultation of the surgeon, who will ask a number of questions concerning the patient’s health, illnesses in the past, the presence of chronic ailments, the taking of any medications and so on.

After this, a standard diagnostic package is assigned, which includes:

  • Laboratory blood tests (general, biochemical, coagulogram), conducted not earlier than 14 days before the date of the appointed liposuction;
  • general urine analysis;
  • Electrocardiogram.

If there are any chronic diseases and / or pathologies, that require continuous medication, it may be necessary to consult with a specialist and his permission to perform the operation.

Postoperative period

After liposuction, the result becomes visible almost immediately, and the skin regains its former elasticity about two months later. For some time after the operation, it is recommended to wear special compression clothes.

The consequences of liposuction can be attributed to a possible slight increase in body temperature for several days. The result of the operation with the further preservation of a stable body weight remains for ever, it is also possible the secondary removal of fat.

How much does liposuction cost?

The cost of surgery can vary significantly, mainly depending on the size of the liposuction zones. The price of the liposuction is also determined by the reliability of the clinic and the qualifications of the staff, as well as the type of anesthesia and, accordingly, the length of the patient’s stay in the hospital.
