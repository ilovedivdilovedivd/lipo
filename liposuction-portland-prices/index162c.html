Features of liposuction in Portland

Indications for liposuction

Indications for liposuction are the following:

  • Local fatty deposits in the region of the hips, abdomen, interblade zone, neck, disturbing the aesthetics of body contours;
  • Arrays of subcutaneous fat, from which it is impossible to get rid by conservative methods (most often they are observed in the face and neck area);
  • Local fat deposits in combination with contraindications to intensive training and / or diets.

During the initial consultation with a plastic surgeon, an assessment is made disproportionate of the body, what fat deposits can be removed, and an individual body correction program is also being prepared. In this case, the patient may be advised to perform a number of other plastic surgeries (abdominoplasty, mastopexy), if the shortcomings of the body or the non-aesthetic contours of the body are due to sagging of the skin and other cosmetic defects. If we need to remove large amounts of body fat, the situation is also treated individually. In this case, other methods for correcting the body may be recommended or a series of liposuction operations may be prescribed.

Contraindications to liposuction

Contraindications for the operation are the following diseases and conditions:

  • diabetes;
  • stomach ulcer;
  • Oncological diseases;
  • phlebeurysm;
  • Some pathologies of the heart (heart failure, heart defects);
  • Obesity of endocrine origin;
  • Hepatic or renal insufficiency.

At primary consultation the doctor can define a number of other contraindications which are of importance in this case. These include the recently transferred viral diseases, the early postpartum period, the use of drugs that alter the blood coagulation function and so on.

It is important to understand, that providing the physician with all the information is one of the key factors that allow liposuction to be carried out to the maximum satisfaction of your expectations and with minimal risk to your health.

Recovery period after surgery

In the early recovery period after liposuction (depending on the peculiarities of the situation, it can be from 1 to 3 weeks), swelling and hematomas may persist. This is a natural postoperative condition, which does not pertain to complications and passes by itself as the tissues recover in the field of intervention.

To prevent undesirable consequences, you will be recommended to wear compression underwear, as well as correction of nutrition and fluid intake, which will speed up the reduction in swelling.

Recommendations regarding physical activity, thermal procedures and other components of your usual life can vary depending on the degree of complexity of the operation and the amount of remote subcutaneous fat.

What determines the cost of liposuction?

Given that for each patient an individual body contour correction program is compiled, the cost may vary to a certain extent. This depends on the complexity of the operation, the amount of subcutaneous fat to be removed, the price of the associated procedures (laser correction of skin defects, abdominoplasty) and other factors.
