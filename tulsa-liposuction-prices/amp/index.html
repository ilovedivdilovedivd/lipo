Tulsa Liposuction Prices

Liposuction / liposculpture (vacuum or ultrasound) in Tulsa is one of the most common procedures to date in aesthetic surgery.

It has been proven that due to the absence of scars in the presence of incredible results, liposuction has become a priority method for removing fat deposits in the thighs, buttocks, sides, shins, and also on the face, neck and arms.

Doctors warn that liposuction is not a method of losing weight, but it is a very effective way to remove excess fat in areas that can not be reduced during exercise or diets. In many patients this can be a genetic problem.

Frequent zones for this operation are the abdomen, sides, hips and knees. In men also performed with gynecomastia (enlarged mammary glands).

These zones are exactly those where liposuction will have an impressive result.

To date, in the arsenal of plastic surgeon there are several methods of liposuction (vacuum, laser and ultrasound, as well as a combination of these techniques). The choice of technique depends on the task the patient poses to the doctor, on the characteristics of the body structure and the expected result.

Liposuction in Tulsa is usually performed under mild general anesthesia and is a “one day” operation. Liposuction is a safe procedure with a very low risk of side effects.

Most patients return to their usual lifestyle during the week, and positive results will be visible in a few days, while the patient will receive the final result in a few months.

How to prepare for surgery?

The first step in the right direction will be a consultation with a plastic surgeon, where he will ask questions about the patient’s state of health and will assign a number of laboratory tests to make sure there are no contraindications. The main tests that you need to have before liposuction:

  • Blood – general, biochemistry and viral diseases, HIV;
  • general urine analysis;
  • cardiogram;
  • consultation with physician and anesthesiologist, if necessary – additional diagnostic measures prescribed by a doctor.

Be sure to tell the surgeon about all medications taken because some of them may adversely affect the result of the operation or lead to complications. In particular, under strict prohibition, everything that liquefies the blood – incl. any form of aspirin and vitamin E.

It will not be superfluous to establish a diet and sleep, and also to give up smoking. But quite often those who will soon have liposuction, sit down on strict diets and give themselves grueling loads in the gym, hoping to get the maximum effect from the operation. It is not right.

The organism will need forces to recover and expose it to unnecessary stresses is extremely undesirable.

A little bit about anesthesia

The surgeon chooses the type of anesthesia depending on the chosen technique, the location and area of the treated area, the individual characteristics of the organism and the wishes of the patient. Therefore the question “what anesthesia is better with liposuction” is not entirely appropriate:

The hardware non-invasive procedures are practically painless, so they do not require any additional drugs. Local anesthesia is used mainly with injection lipolysis. Less often, if the treatment area is not very large, it is also used in surgical operations, but only together with sedative therapy.

Epidural or spinal anesthesia will be optimal for more extensive interventions if the target area is below the waist. With extensive, long-term and complex interventions throughout the body, drug-induced sleep or general anesthesia is used.

How is surgical liposuction performed?

After the anesthetic works, the surgeon starts to work. First of all, he will apply several small incisions on those parts of the body or face, from which it is necessary to remove excess fat.

Usually their length does not exceed a couple of centimeters – just enough to be able to put a cannula under the skin.

Then everything will depend on the type of operation. One way (mechanically, laser, water pressure) destroys fat cells, and the products of their decay, along with the inevitable impurities – blood and other biological fluids – the surgeon pumps out with the cannula.

The larger the target zone, the longer this process takes, the average operation lasts from 30 minutes to 1.5-2 hours.

Then cosmetic sutures are superimposed on all incisions. Moreover, sometimes we can do without them: if the wounds are not too large, they will easily grow together independently, and, in addition, in this case, the inevitable postoperative bruises and puffiness will not be so strong.
