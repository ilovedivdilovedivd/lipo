Houston Liposuction Prices

Liposuction in Houston is a surgical operation aimed not only at removing superfluous fat deposits, but also for correcting the body as a whole.

This method of weight loss is one of the most demanded operations in modern plastic surgery and consists in the local removal of excess fat, that is, it affects not the entire body, but on certain sites.

Liposuction can be carried out in the abdomen, thighs, chin, shins, knees, back and neck.

How is liposuction performed?

Primary consultation of the surgeon.

The doctor listens to the patient’s complaints, collects an anamnesis, tells in detail about the operation, contraindications, appoints the necessary tests.

Secondary consultation.

According to test results, the doctor with the patient agrees to-day operations, says that you need to take with you.

Day of operation.

On the day of surgery, you can not eat. At the appointed time the anesthesiologist checks the patient’s state of health, makes an anesthesia plan. The surgeon at this time makes a markup of the correction zones, and also photographs the patient. The patient wears special clothes.

Operation.

As soon as the anesthesia begins to act (the anesthesiologist does not go anywhere and is always present next to the patient), the surgeon takes the case. After numerous manipulations associated with providing sterile conditions, the surgeon injects into the subcutaneous fat tissue an infiltration solution that will reduce blood loss during surgery.

Then the fat is removed with the help of a cannula, and a special patch is applied from above. The anesthetist outputs from a patient under anesthesia.

After operation.

During the day the patient is under the supervision of doctors, and later your is discharged. The bandage is removed on the same day, and compression underwear is worn in its place.

Many people worry that after liposuction scars will remain forever. This is not true! Traces from the cannula are very tiny, so a couple of months they are completely invisible. There will be swelling, but with the regular wearing of compression underwear and the course of physiotherapy, they will disappear quickly enough.

You can evaluate the result after removing the underwear after two months. The process of the appearance of a new circuit lasts on average about six months. Liposuction can be used in conjunction with other operations, for example, with abdominoplasty.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

  • Hypertension;
  • diabetes;
  • pneumonia;
  • Hepatitis in the acute stage;
  • Gastrointestinal diseases during an exacerbation;
  • tuberculosis.
