Liposuction Cost In Temecula

Liposuction in Temecula is not an alternative to diets for weight loss or physical activity, to keep our body in shape from both functional and aesthetic points of view.

Liposuction in all its manifestations is an additional procedure that is added to those that primarily help to reduce weight (diet) and, secondly, as much as possible to tone the muscles and skin (physical activity).

Summarizing the set of rules that lead to optimal results, we must say that the patient should first lose weight (if there is such a need), strengthening the muscles and giving elasticity to the skin, doing sports.

Only the last resort should be resorted to plastic surgery and, in particular, to liposuction for best results.

Anesthesia during operation of the liposuction

During a specialized examination, the correct assessment of the patient’s condition, the amount of adipose tissue that needs to be removed, and the identification of the most suitable surgical techniques, together with an anesthetic and resuscitation specialist, determine the type and amount of anesthetic needed.

In particular, local anesthesia (with or without deep sedation) is used if liposuction is aimed at eliminating a small amount of material.

If this material is concentrated in the lower part of the body (for example, on the buttocks, hips and legs), the doctor may resort to epidural or spinal anesthesia. Thus, the patient will not feel pain, but will remain conscious.

In two separate cases, it is common to use general anesthesia: when liposuction is aimed at sucking out a large amount of material (here we are talking about megaliposuction) or when the patient himself, who wants to be unconscious in the operating room, requests this type of anesthesia.

During tumescent liposuction (wet lipo), part of the anesthetic (usually lidocaine) is injected into the operated area together with saline and adrenaline (vasoconstrictor).

Surgery for liposuction (liposculpture)

The work of the plastic surgeon and his assistants in modeling the patient’s body begins in the operating room after the onset of anesthesia. The liposuction operation begins with small incisions (2 or 3 mm for tumescent liposuction and a little more for ultrasound) in the operated zone.

These incisions allow the insertion of suction cannulas that are connected to the aspirator or to a large syringe in which a vacuum is created. The removed material enters the graduated capacity, which allows the specialist to accurately control the amount of aspirated material.

Depending on the technique used, the surgeon pushes the cannula to a greater or lesser depth through the subcutaneous fatty tissue. When the desired zone is reached, an injection of the so-called “tumescent cocktail” begins, which, as mentioned, has a vasoconstrictor effect aimed at reducing blood loss.

With the help of “forward-backward” movements, the localized fat is crushed and then sucked off. Even the most modern methods do not allow you to selectively suck out the material.

For this reason, along with fat, a certain amount of fluid, for example, a blood fluid, is also drawn off. Therefore, during the operation on liposuction and / or immediately after it it is necessary to restore balance.

Liposuction in Temecula: postoperative recovery

Upon completion of liposuction surgery patient from the operating room is converted into a specially prepared ward, where he remained for several hours (hospital treatment) in the case of local anesthesia, or until the next day in the case of general anesthesia.

Immediately after the operation, the patient may develop bruises of different degrees, as well as an easy swelling in those areas that have been subject to liposuction. As mentioned above, this is because during the phase of grinding and sucking off the fatty material, some blood vessels are damaged, causing a loss of blood.

These bruises will gradually decrease until a complete disappearance, which usually occurs on average after 2 weeks. After the operation, the pain sensations are insignificant. In any case, they can always be controlled with the usual painkillers.

For better healing of scars and optimization of skin retraction for the first 3 weeks after liposuction, the patient should wear a special supporting bandage that does not limit movement and does not affect the choice of clothing.

Walking is an important assistant in the healing process, so it is recommended to begin to walk moderately on the same day when liposuction was performed, or the next day in case of hospitalization.

You can start working activity within a week, again with moderation, and to engage in sports (in mild form) – in three weeks

Instructions regarding hospital stay and the relatively postoperative period following liposuction or liposculpture are of a general nature. Each person individually, reacts differently to any kind of operation, and plastic surgery is no exception.
