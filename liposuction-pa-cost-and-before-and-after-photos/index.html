INDICATIONS FOR LIPOSUCTION

Indications and contraindications for liposuction in Pennsylvania

Liposuction can be “dry”, when the doctor corrects the contours of the body, removing a small part of the fat, and also moist – the specialist first injects under the skin special solutions that prepare the fat layer to destroy and remove from tissues with the help of laser, vacuum, ultrasound devices.

Wet liposuction in Pennsylvania is performed with significant fat deposits, dry can also be used simply for the purpose of easy correction of the contours of the body, to enhance its aesthetics.

Subcutaneous fat is subject to removal, which can not be eliminated by non-surgical methods (sports, diet) or in the presence of contraindications to active sports training and dietary restrictions.

Liposuction is contraindicated in people who suffer:

  • diabetes mellitus;
  • severe somatic diseases (pneumonia, an open form of tuberculosis, viral hepatitis);
  • malignant tumors;
  • purulent lesions of the skin at the site of local fat removal.

How is liposuction performed?

Before liposuction, the doctor conducts a survey, selects a suitable method for removing fat and makes appropriate notes on the skin. The most gentle methods of liposuction are laser and ultrasound.

Ultrasound causes the effect of cavitation, as a result of which fats take the form of an emulsion, while more solid structures (ligaments, muscle fibers) remain unchanged. Then the fat tissues are removed by means of thin cannulas. This allows to minimize the negative effect on the inner layers of the skin.

Minimally invasive liposuction is preferable to a classical operation, after which patients are often forced to stay for 5-7 days in a hospital.

After ultrasound or laser liposuction, recovery is much faster, the risk of postoperative complications is minimal.

Within a few days of the postoperative period, pain syndrome may persist. The doctor will give the necessary recommendations for care, talk about precautionary rules, appoint a second consultation. If you have any questions, you can ask them to your doctor. Doctors respond quickly to patient problems and are always ready to help.
