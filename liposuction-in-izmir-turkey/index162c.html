What is Liposuction in Izmir?

The formation of a person’s body shape is affected by heredity, and lifestyle, nutrition, ecology and other factors.

Moreover, many people over the years are gaining extra pounds, which again does not adorn the appearance. In addition, it is worth noting that fat is deposited in the walls of internal organs (about a fourth of the total fat mass).

It is important to understand the difference between local fat deposits and the problem of excess weight.

To solve problems with excess weight, as well as obesity, you need to contact dieticians-endocrinologists. The problem of local fat deposits can be effectively solved through surgical intervention.

Liposculpture, lipoplasty and liposuction are synonyms. Liposculpture in Izmir is nothing more than modeling the body by removing excess fatty clusters in local areas (abdomen, things area, flank area). Be that as it may, liposculpture does not replace either diet or exercise. But at the same time, in combination with other corrective procedures (diet, sport, massage), liposuction allows you to achieve the most ideal forms.

About adipose tissue

The localization of surplus fatty tissue is different for men and women. In women, excess fat accumulation is often observed in the buttocks, hips, and chest. In men, most of the excess fat is in the abdomen and neck. With age, the fat mass often increases and shifts to the lower part of the body. All fat tissue of the human body is in three layers – superficial, deep and internal.

The surface layer is under the skin and forms the body relief. It is the surface layer that is corrected during liposuction. The deep layer is located under the fascia of the muscle, it almost does not change when the weight fluctuates. The inner layer is located inside the cavities of the human body. The deep and inner layers are not corrected surgically.

It is believed that the number of fat cells in the body of an adult is unchanged. Obesity is associated with an increase in the volume of fat cells, and not at all. In other words, we can say that with increasing weight, a kind of obesity of the cells themselves occurs.

The procedure of liposuction allows you to reduce the number of fat cells in the most problem areas, but at the same time, the remaining fat cells can increase in volume, if you do not follow your weight. It is important to understand that you can not completely remove fat cells.

Adipose tissue: types and patterns of distribution

Fat tissue is divided into three main types:

Subcutaneous fatty tissue. The subcutaneous layer of fatty tissue determines the smoothness of the outline of the body and is present in all the anatomical areas of the human body. The thickness of subcutaneous fat often determines the way of life and nutrition of a person. Reduce the thickness of this layer can be by exercise and diet.

Deep fat layer. A deep subfascial fat layer is observed in certain anatomical regions (hips, abdomen, chin). Fatty deposits of the deep layer are more a consequence of heredity and when people lose weight they practically do not change.

The internal fat, which is mostly in the abdominal cavity.

In different parts of the body, the thickness and ratio of these layers differ, exerting a significant influence on the contours of the human body. The location and volume of fat deposits, as a rule, depend on sex, age, hereditary predisposition, on metabolism and so on.

It is known that in men and women the regions of distribution of excess fatty tissue differ to some extent.

Excess fatty deposits by the male type are characterized by:

  1. predominantly uniform increase in the thickness of the subcutaneous fat of the limbs and thorax;
  2. significant deposits of adipose tissue in the abdomen, mainly due to internal visceral fat;
    frequent deposition of excess fat in the chin and flanks.

In women, excessive fat deposits most often affect the deep fat layer (hips, abdomen, inner surface of the knee, much less often – the face, lower legs and arms).

Types and forms of body fat

The increase in the volume of adipose tissue occurs due to hypertrophy of fat cells (adipocytes). The consequence of this process is the development of local and general forms of fat deposits.

Local fat deposits

Local hypertrophy of fat cells often occurs due to genetic predisposition. Distinguish the main forms of local fat deposits: fat traps, diffusive-local form and small-hummock contour violations. A feature of all local forms of fatty deposits is the relative stability of their volume and shape, which often do not change even with a significant weight loss.

General obesity is often the result of excessive consumption of food with a relatively sedentary lifestyle. There is hypertrophy of both superficial and deep fat layers. With pronounced obesity, the thickness of the adipose tissue becomes quite significant. Similar changes in most cases occur in adulthood.
