Procedure description:

Vacuum liposuction

It is a pioneer among the other types of fat removal. Vacuum liposuction is performed as follows: through small punctures, a cannula is injected into the subcutaneous fat. Fan-shaped movements the plastic surgeon moves the cannula under the skin, thus destroying the fat cells, which are immediately removed through vacuum suction. With the help of this technique, it is possible to perform liposuction of the abdominal zone, hips, back.

Tumescent liposuction

The method of carrying out this type of liposuction almost does not differ from the vacuum method. The difference is that before the operation, the surgeon introduces into the problem zone a special drug (Klein solution), consisting of physiological solution, anesthetic and vasoconstrictor. As a result, the blood vessels narrow, and the fat cells, on the contrary, swell, which facilitates their further removal. The technique is effective for the abdomen, hips, back.

Electronic lipomodeling

The essence of the method of electronic liposuction is that the fat cells are melted by a high-frequency electric current. To do this, fine needles attached to a generator creating an electric field are introduced into the tissue. The current of high frequency affects the adipose tissue, liquefying it, then the resulting liquid substance is removed from under the skin by aspiration through thin cannulas. Thus, the technique allows to avoid incisions, since for its carrying out only 2-3 small punctures are necessary, imperceptible shortly after the operation.

In addition to eliminating excess fat tissue, electronic lipomodeling solves the second important problem – the correction of excess skin, which invariably occurs during liposuction.

The electric field, in addition to its impact on subcutaneous fat, activates the supply of tissues with oxygen and the metabolism of cells, resulting in increased skin elasticity.

With the help of electronic lipomodeling, liposuction of the abdomen, hips, and small local zones, for example, the second chin, is possible.

The result of liposuction is a slender shape immediately after the operation, with the preservation of the effect for a long period.

Note that liposuction is extremely effective for the removal of existing body fat, but – like any other procedure – is not a panacea for non-compliance with a healthy lifestyle or diet.

However, in practice, patients are so happy with the results from liposuction, they themselves gladly try to save it.

Indications and contraindications for the procedure

Indications:

  • Abdominal fat deposits
  • Fat deposits in the inner thigh area
  • Fat deposits in the buttocks
  • Fat deposits in the hands
  • Fat deposits in the back area
  • Fat deposits in the submandibular zone

Contraindications:

  • Pregnancy and lactation
  • Diabetes
  • Diseases of the circulatory system
