What is liposuction? Why is it needed? What do you need to know about it? How does it differ from the usual methods of losing weight?

These questions are one of the most frequently asked questions.

As a result, in search of better methods of losing weight, a large number of different methods have been developed, many of them have proved ineffective, and some, such as liposuction, on the contrary, have become more and more popular.

This is the surgical removal of excess fat from various areas of the face, body and limbs. Currently, a similar procedure is used throughout the world to give the body beautiful contours.

Under liposculpture is meant a complex medical aesthetic program, which includes the removal of excess fat, those areas where it is needed, as well as ways to restore lost body proportions.

At present, the most modern technologies and equipment are used in liposuction. This method has gained its popularity in the 70’s.

Excess fat deposits were removed by vacuum. The procedure involves the elimination of those fat cells that promote the thickening of the human body. With traditional methods of weight loss, these cells can only be reduced. And liposuction removes them without the possibility of recovery.

What is Liposuction in Buffalo, NY?

This is a full-fledged operation under anesthesia, the essence of which is the cleavage and removal of excess fat, especially from problem areas: the abdomen, thighs, hands or buttocks.

There are many methods of procedure, but all of them are operational – carrying out non-surgical liposuction is impossible by definition, it’s an advertising trick, and nothing more.

Liposuction according to the classical scheme implies the introduction of subcutaneous solutions of adrenaline and saline solution in the area of problem fats. They carry out the splitting of fatty deposits, then through the micro incisions the obtained contents are removed, and the incisions are cosmetically sutured.

Methods of liposuction

Today there are several types of liposuction:

vacuum method, in which the fatty deposits are broken up fragmentarily and sucked off by thin tubes due to the difference in pressure.

with the ultrasonic method, the fat is preliminarily split under the influence of ultrasound of a special frequency and then output through the tubes.

Laser liposuction involves the destruction of fat by laser beams, but the essence remains the same – this is performed under general anesthesia on the operating table.

Choosing a Clinic and Surgeon

Everything would be good in liposuction, but there are a lot of dangers in such an operation. Not all surgeons fully examine their patients before the operation.

In addition, no matter how much you want more, liposuction is just a way to remove existing excess fat, obesity and its causes liposuction does not heal.

If your eating habits and lifestyle do not change after the operation, the hated extra pounds will return in six months, and the weight can become even greater – nature does not tolerate emptiness.

Long-term effects can be expected only if diet, active lifestyle and systematic sports are observed. If you leave everything, as it was, you will have to resort to the help of surgeons once or twice a year – and this is expensive and dangerous for health.

The outcome of the operation largely depends on the surgeon’s qualification and the degree of modernity of the equipment, no clinic will say that its surgeons are bad and they have little experience even if they have done only two operations for the entire time of their existence.

Therefore, the truth will have to be learned independently, according to reviews and personal experience, or to trust doctors and hope for the best. The number of scars, the presence of complications and the speed of recovery during the recovery period depends on the correctness of the operation.

Important is the correct selection for the patient of the plastic surgery technique – alas, liposuction is not indicated to everyone. A self-respecting doctor will appreciate, first of all, the weight of the patient and the degree of stretching of the skin, its ability to recover, because removing the fat and leaving the skin hanging by the bag is not a positive effect of the operation.

If fat is high, most likely, the doctor will refuse to liposuction, offering you a plastic abdomen with the removal of excess tissue. And it is more traumatic and much more expensive.

Do not believe in the promise of simplicity of operation! Liposuction is a serious intervention in the body, and it does not performed without a trace. It is always a stationary procedure and sometimes multi-stage.

You can not simultaneously remove more than 4-5 liters of fat – this leads to a large blood loss (up to 30% of the volume of the removed tissue), since the capillaries thickly adhere fatty tissue. With large amounts of fat, the operation will be performed in several stages with an interval of several weeks or even months.
