How is liposuction performed?

Surgical liposuction in Connecticut is performed most often under general anesthesia. But with liposuction of local areas of the face (cheeks or chin), as well as knees, it is possible to carry out a procedure with local anesthesia.

For liposuction of the hips, as a rule, spinal anesthesia is used. The surgeon performs small (up to 5 mm) incisions, through which special tools are introduced into the area of fat deposits, and begins to destroy the fat layer to be removed. Depending on the size of the area, during liposuction removed from 0.22lb to 26 lb of excess fat.

The procedure of liposuction lasts, on average, about 2 hours, but this number is rather arbitrary and can vary significantly depending on the amount of correction. After surgery, the incision sutured special bioresorbable cosmetic threads.

Recovery period

The patient after liposuction should be under the supervision of doctors from two hours to two days.

An important condition for successful recovery is the wearing of compression underwear after liposuction – patients’ feedback confirms its important role in protecting and supporting the field of surgical intervention, as well as preventing swelling.

The period of wearing such clothes is 4-6 weeks, but the final result of liposuction can be estimated not earlier than in 2 months. The new contour is fully formed within six months.

The patient, if necessary, is assigned a set of rehabilitation measures after liposuction:

  • mesotherapy – to reduce bruising, swelling, bruising elimination;
  • Special massage – to restore blood circulation;
  • Ozone Therapy – for the removal of puffiness and for accelerated recovery of cell health.

Liposuction, depending on the area of intervention, involves some restrictions on physical activity. It is not recommended to visit the sauna, solarium. You should avoid physical activity and stop active for a while.

Possible complications after liposuction in Connecticut

If liposuction is carried out qualitatively and professionally, then, as a rule, complications or undesirable consequences do not happen.

Careful preparation for surgery, good doctor qualification, use of modern equipment, adherence to medical recommendations before and after surgery will all ensure a successful, effective and safe outcome. Possible complications after liposuction can be classified as general and local.

Common complications include:

Fatty embolism is an extremely rare complication that occurs when fat cells enter the bloodstream and then into the capillaries of the lungs. Prevention of fat embolism can be considered a reasonable amount of liposuction.

Postoperative anemia – as a consequence of the loss of a significant volume of blood during surgery. The cause is also the desire to eliminate too much fat tissue at a time.

Local complications after liposuction:

  1. Hematoma due to damage to large blood vessels. It is rare.
  2. Suppuration due to infection of the operating wound. A terrible complication that can lead to a rapid spread of infection. Prevention is compliance with the rules of asepsis and antiseptics.
  3. Seroma (congestion of serous fluid) due to improper work with tissues and the use of cannulas of inappropriate diameters, as well as insufficient compression. Elimination of the problem is achieved through puncture and removal of serous fluid.
  4. Necrosis of the skin due to aggressive removal of subcutaneous fat and damage to the blood vessels, resulting in a violation of the blood supply to the skin and necrosis of its areas. Prevention is the reasonable removal of subcutaneous adipose tissue.

Scarring and pigmentation of scars.

To the complications after liposuction in Connecticut can also be attributed aesthetic defects that do not allow to call the result of the operation fully successful:

  1. Insufficient volume of the removed adipose tissue,
  2. The appearance of a noticeable depression in the area of liposuction in case of excess fat removal,
  3. Uneven contours of the body, the so-called “effect of the washing board.”

Liposuction is an effective way to eliminate fat, but fat deposits can appear again, if the cause of their occurrence is not eliminated – disease or malnutrition. That the problem has not returned, it is necessary to pass necessary treatment and constantly to observe a diet.
