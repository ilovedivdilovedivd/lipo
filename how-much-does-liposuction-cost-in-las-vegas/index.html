With the latest achievements in the field of cosmetology and plastic surgery, women do not necessarily have to wear themselves out with long-term diets to get a beautiful and slender figure.

To give the body the desired contour can be with the help of ultrasonic liposuction.

What is liposuction?

This is the procedure for the destruction of fat cells, which removes subcutaneous fat from the most problematic areas (abdomen, thighs).

Ultrasonic liposuction makes it possible to adjust the contours with minimal surgical intervention during the operation.

Description of the procedure

The procedure involves using the latest ultrasound equipment. Ultrasound acts on a specific site, destroying fat cells under the skin. This kind of liposuction is considered the safest, and also more cost-effective.

Indication for the operation

The main indications for ultrasonic liposuction are:

  • Cellulite on the abdomen and thighs at any stage of manifestation,
  • Local fat deposits,
  • The appearance of scars after removal of fat deposits surgically.

Contraindications

In the process of preparing for the procedure, it is necessary to take into account possible contraindications, which will protect you from the development of complications in the future.

Contraindications include:

  • Pregnancy and lactation,
  • Infectious diseases of a chronic type,
  • oncological diseases,
  • Liver and kidney disease,
  • Period of menstruation,
  • obesity,
  • Presence of implants from metal in the body.

Recovery period

Recovery after liposuction of the hips and abdomen depends on the features of the procedure and is set individually. The general rules of recovery are adherence to a certain diet, restriction of physical activity, as well as strict adherence to the doctor’s instructions in the recovery process, as evidenced by the feedback of our patients.

Possible complications

Ultrasonic procedure is considered the least traumatic type of operation. This feature makes the procedure as safe as possible. Complications that may occur are minor pain, numbness, and swelling in the area of exposure.

Expected results

This type of procedure makes it possible for a short time to find a beautiful figure. Within a few days after the operation, the skin becomes more taut and smooth, the amount of fat deposits decreases in the zone of ultrasound exposure.

An integrated approach to treatment, the implementation of all the instructions of the surgeon will help to consolidate and strengthen the effect. On our site are photos of patients before and after the procedure. Here you will also find customer reviews that have shared their stories of struggle with body fat.

Possible complications of Surgical liposuction

After the operation, it is possible to develop any general surgical complications, as well as the following complications specific to the operation:

  • in very rare cases, the development of an inflammatory process is possible, for the treatment of which an additional operation with skin incisions in the operated region may be required;
  • also a rare complication is the necrosis of the skin in the area of the operation, which sometimes requires repeated operations.

While maintaining a relatively constant weight, the operation gives the optimal result. If the patient is gaining weight, then as with other cosmetic operations, the result is always worse. However, in this case, the place of the former traps, the new fat tissue is deposited more evenly than before liposuction.

Before the procedure / operation you need to undergo examinations or take tests.

Laboratory examination before the procedure / operation you can take place in our clinic, or at the place of residence, depending on the type of anesthesia.

If the operation is performed under general anesthesia, then the following tests should be performed:

  • Common blood and urine tests.
  • Blood test for sugar.
  • Blood type and Rh factor.
  • Coagulogram (blood for clotting).
  • Biochemical analysis of blood.
  • A blood test for the Australian antigen, hepatitis C, HIV.
  • Wasserman’s reaction.
  • Electrocardiogram.
  • The conclusion of the therapist.
  • Fluorography.
  • The conclusion of the gynecologist.

In the operation on the mammary glands this is an U/S and examination by the breast physician.

If the operation is performed under local anesthesia, then the following tests should be taken:

  • The general analysis of a blood + thrombocytes.
  • Blood test for sugar.
  • Clotting time.
  • A blood test for the Australian antigen, hepatitis C, HIV.
  • General urine analysis.
  • Blood type.
  • Fluorography.
  • Electrocardiogram.
