Rhinoplasty – cartilage and skin changes ?
Skin is not removed in rhinoplasty
Oct 5, 2022
Cartilage may be reduced, or often repositioned and contoured. The skin will contract over the underlying modified framework. Skin removal is not a part of cosmetic rhinoplasty.
Saline usage post rhinoplasty – Are there different options?
Saline not required
Oct 5, 2022
Saline is not necessary, plain water will help wash out your nose just fine. When using either you will need to be mindful of sutures inside the nose and take caution not to disrupt them while you are healing.
Taping post splint removal – rhinoplasty
NEVER!
Oct 5, 2022
Never! Tape and bandages cannot shape the nose. That needs to be done during the surgical procedure. The splint I apply stays on for 5 days, my staff and I remove it, and nothing is put on after that.

Best approach to make nose longer?
Nasal lenghtening during Rhinoplasty
Oct 14, 2009
Rhinoplasty to lengthen a nose is usually done by adding cartilage (septal, ear, or rib) extending beyond the end of the septum. These are called septal extension grafts, however, the limiting factor may be skin shortage that limits the degree of improvement. Fillers are a not a great idea for lenghtening the nose so I would not recommend that. Best of luck.

Protruding noses can be corrected by a closed rhinoplasty, or a nose job without external incisions or scars as you describe. In addition, noses can be widened when it is indicated. This is usually done by reducing the height of the nasal cartilage and nasal bones, and adding width to the middle part with spreader grafts, which is cartilage borrowed from the septum.
