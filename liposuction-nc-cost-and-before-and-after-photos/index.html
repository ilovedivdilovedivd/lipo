Liposuction North Carolina Cost And Before And After Photos

Liposuction in North Carolina is a cosmetic surgery, during which excess fat deposits are surgically removed in one or more areas of the body.

What problem areas can be subjected to liposuction?

The experience of the specialists of the medical center, as well as the use of the most modern equipment and techniques, can effectively remove adipose tissue in virtually all areas of the body.

Most often, liposuction is performed on:

– Belly;
– Hips;
– Buttocks;
– Waist;
– Back;
– Forearms;
– Calves.

What are the methods of liposuction?

1. Tumescent liposuction.

The most popular method in the world at the moment, which is due to its minimal trauma. It consists in the fact that a special solution is introduced into the fat layer on the treated area of the body, providing:

  • anesthesia;
  • Vasospasm necessary to reduce blood loss;
  • Dilution of adipose tissue to improve the efficiency of its removal.

After this, a cannula is inserted through a small (up to 1 cm) surgical incision, which is connected to a vacuum device, which sucks up the fat by creating a negative pressure.

Laser liposuction

During this procedure, the fat cells are cleaved by exposing them to laser radiation, which is fed through a special micro-cannula. For its introduction, an incision of only 1-2 mm is required.

An additional advantage of this method is that the laser simultaneously with the destruction of fat provides coagulability of blood in surrounding vessels. In addition, the coherent light radiation stimulates the production of collagen in the skin, producing an effect of its tightening. This method is almost painless.

Ultrasonic liposuction in North Carolina

This method also does not cause painful sensations and is characterized by a very low risk of complications. Directed ultrasonic waves destroy fat cells directly through the skin, which eliminates the need for surgical incisions.Fat, liquefied in this way, is excreted naturally by metabolism in the liver. This is a longer procedure, requiring a course application. Usually, at least 3 sessions are required to achieve the desired result.

What are the contraindications to liposuction?

  1. Chronic diseases in the stage of exacerbation.
  2. General systemic diseases (for example, diabetes mellitus).
  3. Pathology of the circulatory system and blood coagulation system.

How to prepare for liposuction?

  • At least 10 days before the procedure, stop taking any medications that affect blood clotting.
  • At the same time, it is required to refrain from smoking.
  • The specialists of the clinic also recommend to undergo the procedure for cleaning the intestine and to have general clinical laboratory tests.
  • The duration of the liposuction procedure in North Carolina is determined by the scope of the planned intervention. On average, this operation takes from half an hour to 2-3 hours. Most often, liposuction is performed using local anesthesia, but at the request of the patient, our plastic surgeons can perform general anesthesia.

In the postoperative period, there may be some numbness in the areas of the body subjected to liposuction. In addition, sometimes there is bruising. Usually all these symptoms disappear 10-12 days after the intervention.

Surgeons of the medical center are also recommended in the first few weeks after liposuction to restrict intense physical activity and exercise.
